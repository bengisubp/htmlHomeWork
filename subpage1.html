German Grammar

Nouns and Articles

In German, all nouns are capitalized and have a gender: masculine, feminine, or neuter. The definite articles are "der" for masculine, "die" for feminine, and "das" for neuter.

Verb Conjugation

German verbs are conjugated to reflect the person and number of the subject. For example:

  • ich bin (I am)
  • du bist (you are)
  • er/sie/es ist (he/she/it is)
  • wir sind (we are)
  • ihr seid (you all are)
  • sie sind (they are)

Sentence Structure

The basic sentence structure in German is subject-verb-object, but the verb is often the second element in the sentence.
